Biography of Anthony Weiner

Anthony Weiner was born on September 4, 1964, in Brooklyn, New York. He graduated from the State University of New York at Plattsburgh with a Bachelor of Arts degree in political science.

After serving as an aide to then-U.S. Representative Chuck Schumer, Weiner was elected to the New York City Council in 1991, representing the 48th district in Brooklyn. He served on the council until 1998, when he was elected to the United States House of Representatives, representing New York’s 9th congressional district.

During his time in Congress, Weiner was known for his outspoken and often combative style. He was a strong advocate for progressive causes such as universal healthcare and workers’ rights, and he frequently clashed with conservatives on issues like immigration and national security.

Weiner gained national attention in 2011 when he was caught up in a sexting scandal. After initially denying that he had sent explicit photos to women online, he eventually admitted to the behavior and resigned from Congress.

In 2013, Weiner ran unsuccessfully for mayor of New York City, but his campaign was derailed by further revelations about his past sexting behavior.

In 2016, Weiner again became embroiled in a sexting scandal, this time involving a minor. He pleaded guilty to charges related to the incident and was sentenced to 21 months in prison.

Weiner is now retired from politics and has focused on other pursuits, including writing and consulting work. Despite his past controversies, he remains a polarizing figure in American politics.
